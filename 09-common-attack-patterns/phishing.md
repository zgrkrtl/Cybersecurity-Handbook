# Phishing – Quick Notes

## What is Phishing?

Phishing is a social engineering attack where an attacker  
tries to trick a victim into:

- Revealing sensitive information
- Clicking a malicious link
- Downloading malware
- Approving fraudulent requests

It usually impersonates a trusted entity (bank, company, colleague).

---

# How Phishing Works

1. Attacker prepares fake message
2. Message is sent via email / SMS / social media
3. Victim interacts (clicks, downloads, submits credentials)
4. Attacker gains access or data

Goal → Steal credentials, money, or gain initial access.

---

# Common Types of Phishing

## 1. Email Phishing
- Fake invoice
- Password reset request
- Account suspension warning

Most common form.

---

## 2. Spear Phishing
- Targeted at a specific person
- Uses personal information
- Looks more realistic

Often used against executives or IT admins.

---

## 3. Whaling
- Targets high-level executives (CEO, CFO)
- Often financial fraud related

---

## 4. Smishing
- Phishing via SMS
- Fake delivery message
- Bank alert with malicious link

---

## 5. Vishing
- Voice phishing (phone call)
- Fake IT support
- Fake bank representative

---

# Common Techniques Used

- Domain spoofing (lookalike domains)
- URL shorteners
- Fake login pages
- Attachment with malicious macro
- Urgency language ("Act now!")
- Authority pressure ("CEO request")

---

# Indicators of Phishing

- Suspicious sender address
- Misspelled domain name
- Unexpected attachment
- Urgent or threatening tone
- Mismatch between link text and actual URL
- Requests for credentials or OTP codes

---

# Technical Impact

Phishing can lead to:

- Credential theft
- Account takeover
- Malware infection
- Business Email Compromise (BEC)
- Ransomware deployment

---

# Prevention Measures

User-side:
- Verify sender address
- Hover over links before clicking
- Do not share OTP codes
- Report suspicious emails

Organization-side:
- Email filtering
- DMARC / SPF / DKIM
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- Security awareness training
- Conditional access policies
